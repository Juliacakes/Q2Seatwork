CAT BREEDS

British Longhair A medium sized, short, muscular cat breed, with a wide chest. Underneath an impressive straight, resilient and weather-proof coat, there is a compact round paws.
Ragdoll A highly intelligent cat. With a bit of patience, it's possible to teach them basic tricks like 'fetch' and 'roll over'. Despite their easy-going nature.
Scottish Fold A breed of domestic cat with a natural dominant gene mutation,causing the ears to "fold", which gives the cat what is often described as an "owl-like" appearance.
Bombay cat A short-haired breed of domestic cat, closely related to the Burmese. Bombay cats are typically characterized as having an all-black coat, black soles, black nose and mouth, with copper or green eyes.
American Bobtail Alarge, deep-set eyes that are almond-shaped and can come in any color. They have long back legs and a large, These cats are sometimes slow maturing and can take longer than your typical cat to reach full adulthood.
Balinese cat A medium-sized cat with a long, tubular body. The head is a long triangle and the ears are set so they appear to continue the triangle.
Munchkin cat A walnut-shaped, wide-set eyes which, like their coat, can come in almost any colour.
Russian Blue A thick, gorgeous blue-gray coat and bright eyes. These cats are sweet, loyal, and cautious animals who love having a routine.
Manx Cat A medium-sized cat but she is stocky and heavily boned. The Manx is a rounded cat with a round head, round eyes, a roundness at the whisker pads, and a round rump.
Somali cat A semi-long-haired cat, with fur lying shorter and closer on the body, and longer around the neck, legs and tail. This gives the cat a shaded appearance, paler underneath and darker on top.
